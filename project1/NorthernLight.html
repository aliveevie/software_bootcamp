The Northern Light

THE OTHERWORLDLY AURORA
borealis, or northern lights,
begin high in the Earth’s
atmosphere—at altitudes from
60 to more than 250 miles—
when charged particles from the
sun become trapped in the
Earth's magnetic field. The result
is a colorful, dancing light show.



The time I filmed on aurora was from an icebreaker near antarctica. Luckily there are much more accessible places if you like to take in these light show yourself. for the best in the celestial scene consider anywhere with the magnetic latitude above 55degree and low light pollution. find your magnetic latitude on NOAA and here

In the polar latitude, aurora can appear on any dark night. Long winter night are good but not necessarily the best time. Near equinox in Match and September, the earth magnetic field lets more solar particles interact with the atmosphere, creating aurora season! I suggest automnal equinox in september, when are there present temperatures in polar latitude. find the dark hours of your location here, or by using a skyguide app.
